Questions about aeration.

In Central Florida, spring (March–April) and fall (September–October) are ideal. Aerating during active growth gives the lawn the best chance to recover and fill in quickly.

The soil plugs break down within 2–4 weeks. Overseeded areas typically germinate within 1–3 weeks. Full results — thicker turf, better drainage — are visible within 4–8 weeks.

Once per year is typically sufficient for most residential lawns. High-traffic yards or heavily compacted soil may benefit from twice-annual aeration.

For best results, avoid heavy foot traffic for 1–2 weeks after overseeding. Light watering twice daily for the first two weeks helps seed germination significantly.
